A Historical Perspective:
The auto rickshaw, often colloquially referred to as the "auto" or "tuk-tuk," made its debut in India in the mid-20th century. Introduced as a means to bridge the gap between cycle rickshaws and full-sized taxis, the auto rickshaw quickly gained popularity for its maneuverability and cost-effectiveness. Over the years, it evolved into a ubiquitous presence on India's roads, adapting to the diverse needs of commuters in both urban and rural areas.
